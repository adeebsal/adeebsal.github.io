My understanding of interdisciplinarity honestly hasn't changed a lot through this course, but has become deeper. In the beginning of the semester I said that to me, interdisciplinarity refers to being able to factor differing perspectives into decisions, learning, and finding solutions. In this course, I have learned that instead of simply factoring in other disciplines, interdisciplinarity means to actually draw from those disciplines to create new meaning and find new insights that wouldn't be possible otherwise. Simply bridging multiple disciplines to look at a problem from many perspectives toes the line between multidisciplinarity and interdisciplinarity, but creating new meaning makes an endeavor firmly interdisciplinary. I also learned that to correctly bridge disciplines for an interdisciplinary study, one must modify concepts and/or assumptions from disciplines to find common meaning, rather than just looking at the concepts from different disciplines to find common ground.

In my career, I plan to use the skills I've learned in this class to solve problems more effectively with the technology I work on. The ultimate goal of any form of technology is to solve a problem or address a need, and an interdisciplinary understanding of the problem will allow me to better fit my projects to solve the problem at hand.
